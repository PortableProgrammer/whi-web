SSTray

Tired of that ugly lock screen? Wish your screensaver would start automatically when you lock your workstation? Rather your monitor(s) power down instead?

Download SSTray 2.1 »

SSTray allows you to choose what happens when you lock your workstation. You can even start the screen saver or power down the monitors independent of locking your workstation.

Known Issues

When a password is required to dismiss the screensaver, Windows doesn't lock the workstation until after the screensaver is dismissed.
If the screensaver starts automatically, SSTray interprets the lock action after screensaver dismissal as a new lock request, and starts the screensaver.

Recommended Workaround

Set SSTray to "Start Screensaver" upon lock and use the Win+L shortcut to manually lock the workstation.
Other Workarounds Set SSTray to "Do Nothing" upon lock and use the Win+S shortcut to start the screensaver manually.

Requirements
